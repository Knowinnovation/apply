Overview

NSF is creatively exploring HOW we fund science. This series of workshops are designed to produce novel-to-NSF funding frameworks that support science, engineering, and education. NSF is exploring research space that requires a conceptual switch; NSF currently support the “pursuit of basic scientific research that could benefit society”, but in the future NSF may want to “Benefit society through the pursuit of basic scientific research that allows X in Y years.”

With that conceptual switch in mind, how would a funding framework look if NSF defined an end state that has specific societal benefits? How might we support research that would get us to a specified end state in a specific timeframe? If NSF knows the desired societal impacts, and has a timeline for reaching them, how do we fund the intellectual merit to get there?

These workshops are designed to facilitate out of the box thinking (i.e. imagine no budget limitation and no PAPPG!). The workshops are scientifically agnostic because we are interested in the how and not the what of funding frameworks. This opportunity is open to ALL NSF staff, regardless of type of position. In particular, science assistants, program support staff, grants management specialists and fellows are encouraged to attend, and all ideas are welcome. We need a diversity of participants from across NSF! Attend a single workshop or attend multiple workshops, there is an opportunity for ideas and feedback from all to be heard!

Open to All. These sessions will serve to bridge gaps. We will spend time further developing and refining the opportunity concepts.
