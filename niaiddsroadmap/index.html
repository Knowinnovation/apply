Overview

The Office of Data Science and Emerging Technologies (ODSET) at The National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Disease (NIAID) will conduct a series of data science Innovation Labs in the biomedical fields of infectious diseases, emerging infections, and immune-mediated diseases including allergy, autoimmunity, and immune reactions associated with transplantations. The purpose of the series will be to convene experts and stakeholders working toward advancing data science for discovery and innovation in understanding, treating, preventing, and responding to outbreaks of infectious and immune-mediated diseases per the mission of NIAID. The primary aim will be to draw from community expertise in this domain to build a roadmap for data science at NIAID.

Through the series, participants will provide insights into the current landscape of data science research and development, as well as questions and ideas that are expected to shape the future of data-driven immune-mediated and infectious disease research. Participants invited from basic, applied and clinical research from both academia and industry will represent a variety of fields including computer science, biomedicine, and ethics. The Innovation Lab series will provide foundational materials that will help to inform NIAID’s prospective role in data science program development and serve as a platform for collaboration, idea generation, and networking for participants. Insights generated in the series will be distilled into a roadmap for data science at NIAID. Such a roadmap will guide the next five years of data science program development that NIAID can leverage to accelerate work in diagnosing, preventing, monitoring, and treating infectious and immune-mediated disease.

Key Objectives

  • Identify research and translation gaps that can be addressed by data science within the NIAID mission
  • Provide guidance on key infrastructure for effective data science research and innovation
  • Develop performance metrics for the state of the science
  • Foster a community of researchers & stakeholders
  • Adopt open & team science to accelerate advancement and enhance impact
  • Sensitizing the field to ethics, privacy, inclusivity, and policy

Key Deliverables

  • Annotated notes from data science stakeholders
  • A commentary paper outlining NIAID’s roadmap for data science to be submitted to a high-impact peer-reviewed scientific journal (based on annotated notes, above)
  • A set of infographic and other material resources for highlighting and promoting data science at NIAID
