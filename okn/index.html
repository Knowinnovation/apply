Overview

OKN, the open knowledge network, is a project that envisions connecting many public government datasets into one large graph-style database. The benefit of this is that government agencies, private organizations, and academics can better utilize data generated or managed by the government.

The project's current focus is to construct and develop compelling use cases to illustrate what might be possible with a fully functional open knowledge network.

Stakeholders

This project will require input from the broadest collection of scientific disciplines, as well as input from all types of government agencies that publish open data (which is nearly all of them).

In addition to academic and government participants, industry partners are key stakeholders. If an open knowledge network is assembled and made available, some of the biggest beneficiaries stand to be private and public companies small and large, and they should have a say from the start.

The Opportunity

This event series is unlike your typical top down speaker/panel dominated conferences.

  • The project is interested in real problems and novel solutions.
  • This is a chance to shape the development of how a major technological resource develops.

Meeting Outcomes

The work done will produce an integrated report, outlining key opportunities and challenges with utilizing an open knowledge network.
