Use Cases For The Open Knowledge Network

Over the last four months, interdisciplinary groups have come together to explore potential use cases of an Open Knowledge Network as a way of illustrating its potential impact. Let's explore those use cases...

To engage directly with the use case group members, feel free to join our Slack community.


Strategic Supply Chain Resiliency

Cavan Paul Capps, Vinay Chaudhri, Melissa Cragin, Jennifer Dunn, Jeff Heflin, Michael Moore, Emanuel Sallinger, Dalia Varanka, Krzysztof Janowicz, Matthew Lange

An OKN could enable us to have near-real time visibility into supply chain flows for strategic commodities and goods, enabling a variety of user groups to collectively evaluate the health of their industry, inclusing achieving supply chain resilience.

Integrated Justice Platform

Alexia C., Ashwini J., Christina M., Kat A., Luis A., Paul W., Sam C., Adam P.

The US has more incarcerated people than any nation, many of whom remain unemployed, end up homeless, or worse. If we could collect, aggregate, and harmonize data across the subsystems comprising the justice system we could improve the way it works, and learn how different individuals move through the system at large.

Innovation Ecosystem Mapping and Alignment

Jemin George, Nathan Burkholder, Zeke Topolosky, Jed Pitera, Florence Hudson, Louiqa Raschid, Murat Omay, Ying Ding

We believe an OKN can be used to map and align Department of Defense innovation programs for industry and government users to more efficient engage with DoD opportunities, savings millions of dollars and a lot of time.

National Information Exchange Model Ontology

Tom Carlson, Katherine Escobar, Pascal Hitzler, Cogan Shimizu, Dave Hardy, Ora Lassila

Underlying Premise: NIEM is a common vocabulary (ontology), enabling broad interoperability between domain-specific knowledge graphs. This is a prerequisite for building an Open Knowledge Network.

Connecting Investments in Nutrition Security and Climate

Ann Stapleton, Ed Chow, Pascal Hitzler, John MacMullen, Sudip Bhattacharjee, Ilya Zaslavsky, Shashi Shekhar, Matthew Lange, Krzysztof Janowicz, Ellie Young, Sharat Israni

Today, we have no scalable method to connect understanding of food system relevant data investments across federal departments and agencies beyond interagency working group person-to-person communication. A food and climate open knowledge network (OKN) has the potential to vastly scale up our understanding of what is known about federal investments, which is key to supporting our interagency efforts and in meeting White House all-of-government priorities.

An Open Knowledge Network for Health Communication Management

Nariman Ammar, Irma Arispe, Chengkai Li (point person), Brian Handspicker, Florence Hudson, Katerine Osatuke, Mark Schildhauer (back-up point person)

We propose to build an OKN of health-related information and misinformation for health communicators and citizens to better understand the landscape of online health communication and to create and discover effective intervention messages.

Enabling Community Climate Action and Local-Federal Intelligence Transmission

Ellie Young, Stan Ahalt, Ashley Atkins, Brian Blanton, Chris Bizon, Johannes Friedrich, Jose Fortes, Matt Gee, Bandana Kar, SJ Klein, Chris Lenhardt, Ryan McGranaghan, Jorrit Poelen, Douglas Rao, Lilit Yeghiazarian, Ilya Zaslavsky

We propose building a multi-faceted decision-support system to deliver coherent climate data for local community residents and decision makers to manage community infrastructure, health, equity, biodiversity, resilience, and so forth. To deliver a complete support package for local change initiatives, we propose to augment this software system with a connected ecosystem of data providers and experts, enabling collaborative communications between cross-sector groups.

Homelessness knowledge network for cities

Jan Aasman, Ying Ding, Tim Goliver, Dina Hooshyar (VA), Sam Klein, Sarah E, Eric Jahn, Sonia Kim, William Snow (HUD)

We propose an OKN of real-time data tracking homelessness, available housing, usage of shelter and social services, and related funding and programs. This will help city and community leaders to house every citizen and prevent future homelessness, with visibility into how their city is doing, and the dynamic flows maintaining that equilibrium.

Electronic consent services to be used as a common mechanism to manage privacy preferences and consent assertions across ontology-driven systems

Brian Handspicker, Eric Jahn, Esther Jackson, Katerine Osatuke, Kenneth Berkowitz, Luis Amaral, Paul Wormeli, Noam Arzt, Camille Nebeker, Cori Thompson (Drummond Group) Jim Pasquale (Kantara), Kristine McCoy

We propose building an electronic consent service to be used as a common mechanism to manage privacy preferences and consent assertions for protection of personal and/or sensitive information that is requested to be shared between ontology-driven systems. This service will achieve improved availability and appropriate protection of sensitive information (which may otherwise be withheld due to law, regulation, policy, procedure, or simply uncertainty by those holding that information).

Decision Support for Government Leaders

Todd Bacastow, Michel Biezunski, Jose Fortes, Tim Goliver, Oktie Hassanzadeh, Nicholas Maynard, Murat Omay, Amanda Stathopoulos, Glenn Ricart

We propose building a Data Integration and Prediction OKN Model primarily based on federal, state, and local data already being collected for decision-makers at the federal, state, and local levels and their constituents to achieve better, data-based governance decisions by developing a strategic decision-support tool which (a) provides decision-makers data-driven insight and (b) predicts the range of expected impacts for any given decision.

Collaborative Knowledge Graph Network

Greg Seaton, He Bai, Ken Wright, Carl Busart, Quyen Nguyen, Florence Hudson, Ilya Zaslavsky, Jemin George, Nick Del Rio, Emily Rothenberg, Lauren Close

We propose integrating disparate datasets, data sources and knowledge graphs from different organizations to coordinate policy development and planning. Not constrained to a single domain, this will provide horizontal integration to provide greater insight within medicine, energy policy, etc. (i.e. not constrained to a single stove-piped domain)

OKN for Energy Management (Achieve 100% Renewable and Resilience)

Johannes Friedrich, Jianxi Gao, Matt Gee, Ted Habermann, Bandana Kar, Ryan McGranaghan, Jennifer Melot, Douglas Rao, Ellie Young, Lilit Yeghiazarian

We propose developing a multi-faceted decision support tool that would allow DOE, utilities and local communities/stakeholders to understand the current risk to the grid, the relationship between communities and the grid system from the perspective of energy justice and burden, identify potential places for renewables to address the supply and demand.

Decarceration and Reunification From Prison Incarceration to Community Reintegration and Family Reunification

Brian Handspicker, Eric Jahn, Christina Medlin, Sonia Kim, Daniel Stein, Kristine McCoy, Kenneth Berkowitz, Katherine Escobar, Stephen Sullivan, Thomas Carlson, Jennifer Hall, David Hardy

We propose building an Incarceration to Community Decarceration Service Planning system to support identification and delivery of social services needed to enable successful return to community and family life for incarcerated users to achieve reduced recidivism and improved well-being of both the formerly incarcerated individuals and their family members.

Learning Resources for OKN Technologies

Cogan Shimizu, Esther Jackson, Florence Hudson, Bandana Kar, Emanuel Sallinger, Emily Rothenberg, Lauren Close, Ora Lassila, Nariman Ammar

This OKN Use Case will act as an entry point to the rest of the OKN, by providing a unified, foundational learning platform. The different aspects and initiatives of the OKN will use a wide variety of different tools and technologies, many of which will leverage fundamental concepts that are likely new to many of those involved. These concepts; the tools that are built on top of them; the frameworks they employ or comprise; and the use cases that they solve will all need to be described in an understandable and approachable manner, so that the OKN may best serve the public good and facilitate true public access.

Compounding Disasters/Events Use Case AKA Wicked Problems Institute

Ryan McGranaghan, Christine Kirkpatrick, Krzysztof Janowicz, Ashley Atkins, Michel Biezunski, Florence Hudson, Jeff Heflin, Silviu Cucerzan, Todd Bacastow, Douglas Rao, Mark Schildhauer, Lilit Yeghiazarian, Ilya Zaslavsky, Ellie Young, Sharat Israni

We propose creating an information commons that takes advantage of existing OKN efforts that address specific risks (e.g., floods (UFOKN), fires (KnowWhereGraph), space weather and terrestrial weather storms (CHESS)) and integrative tools (the COVID Information Commons, EarthCube and Big Data Hubs) to bring them together to understand the compounding effects of risks and give context and create situational awareness to any given risk.

Public Risk Analysis & Alert System powered by a financial OKN

Sam Chance, Vinay Chaudhri, Victor Chen, Ying Ding, Oktie Hassanzadeh, Jay Pujara, Louiqa Raschid, Emanuel Sallinger, Ellie Young

A public service/tool that provides indicators and indices around risk factors (negative) and opportunities (positive) for industries / sectors. The service is powered by a business and finance OKN curated from publicly available sources (e.g. SEC filings, analyst reports), news reports, Web text, etc. A technical challenge is turning unstructured and structured data into useful knowledge.

Bias in Research Priorities Knowledge Monitor

Luis Amaral, Matthew Lange, Douglas Rao, Jennifer Dunn, Adam Pah, Ilya Zaslavsky, Ying Ding, Jianxi Gao

We propose the development of an open knowledge network that enumerates and characterizes contemporary and historical sources of bias/gaps and how they relate to scientific funding streams and outcomes with the goal of identifying overlooked research approaches and facilitating more equitable and robust scientific funding allocation that takes into consideration, and perhaps addresses historical, disciplinary, and technological biases of scientific funders.
