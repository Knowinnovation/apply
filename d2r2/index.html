What Is An Ideas Lab?

An Ideas Lab is a 5-day event for an interdisciplinary group of scientists working to develop innovative research concepts for a wicked hard problem.

There are no lengthy keynotes or panels, no symposia or poster sessions. This will be an immersive, dynamic event in which you'll be intellectually and creatively challenged while you generate, explore, and develop unique, ambitious research concepts.

Join a 5-day event to develop novel cross-disciplinary collaborations!

Overview

The mystery of how organisms' genomes produce the variety of phenotypes observed in nature is at a profound an interesting place. Decades of work and discovery have helped us understand how some RNA, transcribed from DNA, are used to encode proteins, which then fold into structures that provide various functions to an organism. This RNA however, only accounts for a fraction of the total. There is another major segment of an organism's DNA, which is transcribed to RNA in the cell, but does not encode for proteins.

What do Non-coding RNA do? And how? This is the focus of our Ideas Lab.
